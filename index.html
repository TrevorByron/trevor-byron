My Process

The steps I take in my design process

Project Research

I believe that good design comes from good research. Research is the cornerstone and building block of every design desicion that follows.

Wireframes

Low fidelity mockups, whether as sketches or wireframes, allows for more iterations at faster pass. The more we iterate the better the final outcome.

Design

Using, primarily, Photoshop and Sketch I'm able to get layouts, colors, fonts and sizing out of my head and into a high fidelity mockups. This is the bread and butter

Code

As a designer I have the benefit of having a good handle on the development process. This streamlines the communication between myself and the development team.

My Background

International/Creative/Social Science/Music






Trevor Byron is an Austin based UI/UX designer. Trevor was born in Nairobi, Kenya and spent his growing up years in a remote Maasai village in the southern highlands called the Loita Hills. Trevor's introduction to color and design was learning traditional bead work, making necklaces and bracelets with his Maasai babysitter. Trevor designed his first website in 2005 for a punk-rock band he was a part of. After graduating from Westmont College with a degree in Social Science Trevor dove into creative work, making videos, designing logos and hacking together Squarespace websites for artists and non-profits. He also apprenticed as a high-end wood worker doing Finish Carpentry in Santa Barbara, CA. As his creative experiences developed he started honing in on his design skills and in early 2017, Trevor graduated from an intensive 3 month training in UI Design from the Iron Yard Academy in Austin.
